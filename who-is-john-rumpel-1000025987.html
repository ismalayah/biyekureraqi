John Rumpel Plane Crash

On June 4, 2023, a tragic incident occurred when a Cessna passenger aircraft, owned by John Rumpel, a prominent MAGA donor and Florida businessman, crashed into Virginia Woods. John Rumpel has confirmed that his entire family, including his daughter, two-year-old grandchild, and her nanny, were on board the plane at the time of the crash.

The Cessna was registered to Encore Motors, owned by John and Barbara Rumpel of Melbourne, Florida. Regrettably, no survivors were found at the crash site. The sequence of events began when the Cessna aircraft, which was en route to Long Island MacArthur Airport in New York, entered restricted U.S. airspace over Washington, D.C.

This prompted an aerial pursuit involving Pentagon jets, as the pilot of the Cessna was unresponsive. During the pursuit, the Pentagon jets went supersonic, causing a sonic boom that reverberated across the Washington D.C. area.

The North American Aerospace Defense Command (NORAD) confirmed that the jets intercepted the out-of-control aircraft at 3:20 pm on Sunday. NORAD clarified that the jets were authorized to travel at supersonic speeds, which could explain the sonic boom reported by residents in the region.

Officials stated that one of the jet pilots confirmed that the Cessna pilot had lost consciousness during the chase. As a result, the plane ultimately crashed into mountainous terrain near the George Washington National Forest in Virginia around 3:30 pm on Sunday. Despite the immediate response from first responders, the search for survivors was halted on Sunday night after no survivors were discovered at the crash site, as reported by The Daily Beast.
